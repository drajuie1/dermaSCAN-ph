📚 Learn: MPOX vs Chickenpox

Understand symptoms, spread, and what to do

🦠

MPOX (Monkeypox)

Caused by the Monkeypox virus · First found in humans in 1970

🤒 Symptoms

Swollen lymph nodes High fever (before rash) Deep, firm lesions All lesions same stage Face → hands/feet → body Muscle aches Exhaustion

📡 How It Spreads

  • Close skin-to-skin contact
  • Contact with infected animals
  • Prolonged respiratory droplets
  • Contaminated bedding or clothing
  • NOT as contagious as chickenpox

✅ What To Do

  • ISOLATE immediately
  • Call DOH Hotline: 1555
  • Cover all lesions with bandages
  • Wear a mask at all times
  • Do NOT share personal items
  • Antiviral treatment is available

🚨 See a Doctor Immediately If...

  • Difficulty breathing or swallowing
  • Rash spreads to eyes
  • Fever above 39°C (102°F)
  • Rapidly spreading lesions
  • You are pregnant or immunocompromised
💧

Chickenpox (Varicella)

Caused by Varicella-Zoster virus · Very common in children

🤒 Symptoms

Very itchy rash Multiple stages at once Fluid-filled blisters Mild to moderate fever Starts on chest/back Headache Loss of appetite

📡 How It Spreads

  • Airborne — highly contagious
  • Direct contact with blister fluid
  • Coughing or sneezing
  • Contagious 1–2 days BEFORE rash
  • Until ALL blisters crust over

✅ What To Do

  • Stay home and rest
  • Do NOT scratch blisters
  • Calamine lotion for itching
  • Paracetamol for fever (NOT aspirin)
  • Loose cotton clothing
  • Ask about varicella vaccine

🚨 See a Doctor Immediately If...

  • Rash spreads to eyes
  • Rash is red, warm, or has pus
  • High fever above 39°C
  • Adult, pregnant, or elderly
  • Newborn has symptoms
